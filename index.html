What does the NPC do?

  • Provides recommendations to the U.S. Secretary of Energy
  • Conducts in-depth studies on key energy issues
  • Brings together a cross-section of the energy community — industry, academic, environmental, Tribal, and public-interest voices

134th Meeting of the National Petroleum Council

Date: Tuesday, April 23, 2024  |  Location: Washington, DC

The Council met in Washington, D.C., to receive briefings and discuss ongoing studies requested by the Secretary of Energy, including work on hydrogen, greenhouse gas emissions, and near-term supply considerations.

Recent Reports Approved by the Council

Explore recently approved Council reports that address critical issues in the nation’s energy system.

Reliable Energy: Gas–Electric Coordination

This study examines how growing interdependence between natural gas and electric power systems affects reliability and resilience. It outlines challenges in market alignment, scheduling, and communication, offering practical recommendations to improve coordination and support reliable, affordable energy delivery.

View report

Project Permitting Innovation

This report examines how rising U.S. energy demand is colliding with an outdated permitting system, increasing costs and constraining essential infrastructure. It outlines targeted reforms and introduces a new “Qualified Infrastructure Authorizations” framework to streamline approvals while maintaining strong environmental protections.
