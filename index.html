What does the NPC do?

  • Provides recommendations to the U.S. Secretary of Energy
  • Conducts in-depth studies on key energy issues
  • Brings together a cross-section of the energy community — industry, academic, environmental, Tribal, and public-interest voices

Leadership Announcement

NPC Announces Appointment of Next Executive Director

The National Petroleum Council has announced the appointment of its next Executive Director. The Executive Director leads the Council's staff in coordinating study efforts and supporting Council meetings, ensuring that the Secretary of Energy receives timely, well-documented advice on oil and natural gas issues.

Policy News

NPC to Initiate Study on U.S. Energy Transition Pathways

The Council will launch a comprehensive new study at the request of the Secretary of Energy, focusing on infrastructure requirements, technology readiness, and policy considerations for a secure and reliable energy transition over the next decade.

Upcoming 135th NPC Meeting

Council to Convene in Washington, D.C.

The 135th meeting of the National Petroleum Council will be held in Washington, D.C., with members and guests invited to discuss ongoing studies and emerging issues affecting the U.S. energy system. A live webcast will be available for remote viewers.

Study Spotlight

Hydrogen for a Lower-Carbon Future

NPC Examines Role of Hydrogen in Energy Transition

An NPC study team is evaluating how hydrogen can support a lower-carbon energy system, including opportunities in industry, power generation, and transportation. The work reviews technology readiness, infrastructure needs, and policy options to scale deployment.
